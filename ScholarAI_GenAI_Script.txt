ScholarAI GenAI Presentation Script

Slide 1 — Title
"This is ScholarAI, our GenAI class project. We built a RAG plus multi-agent platform for assistive learning, focused on what happens after assessment."

Slide 2 — Problem
"The core problem is the post-quiz learning gap. Students usually get a score, but not structured guidance. Instructors also need scalable ways to review and coach each student."

Slide 3 — GenAI Goal
"Our goal is to use grounded GenAI to convert quiz outcomes into learning actions: diagnosis, explanation, and personalized guidance."

Slide 4 — Method Overview
"We combine two ideas: RAG for grounded context and an agentic pipeline for task-specific generation. RAG reduces hallucination risk; agents separate logic for answer, planner, quiz, and explanation modes."

Slide 5 — Architecture
"Frontend is Next.js with role-aware UX. Backend is FastAPI orchestrating multi-agents. Firebase Auth handles identity and Firestore stores workflow state including invites, assignments, and submissions."

Slide 6 — Key GenAI Features
"We support RAG-grounded chat, AI study planning, AI quiz generation, and answer explanation. Each uses context retrieval plus mode-specific prompt structure."

Slide 7 — Assessment Workflow
"Professor assigns quizzes. Student answers and submits. The system grades and stores structured submission data. Professor can inspect each student submission directly."

Slide 8 — Personalization
"Submission signals help identify weak areas. This enables targeted feedback and supports generating focused study plans for each student, not one-size-fits-all advice."

Slide 9 — Results / Behavior
"The system supports both LMS-guided learning and independent learning. Students can explore any topic personally, while course topics remain instructor-structured."

Slide 10 — Evaluation Lens
"We evaluate groundedness, end-to-end workflow completion, usability across roles, and reliability of topic-owner/content resolution."

Slide 11 — Limitations
"Current grading has heuristic matching limits, quality depends on uploaded materials, and analytics depth is still limited."

Slide 12 — Future Work
"We plan semantic grading, automatic remediation plans, richer instructor analytics, and direct LMS integrations."

Slide 13 — Takeaway
"This project shows practical GenAI system design: not just generation, but grounded generation integrated into real learning workflows and decision loops."

Q&A bridge
"I can now show a live demo: professor assignment flow, student submission, and instructor-side review with adaptive next-step support."
